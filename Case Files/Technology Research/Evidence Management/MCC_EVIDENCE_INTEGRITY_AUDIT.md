## SECTION 1: MCC CAMERA SYSTEM CHRONOLOGY

### July 2019: Initial System Failures

**Timeline:**
- **July 29, 2019:** DVR motherboard failure
- **Impact:** Majority of cameras in Special Housing Unit (SHU) stopped recording
- **Known issue:** For months, roughly half of the facility's cameras had not been recording
- **Consequence:** SHU video gap extended (pre-Aug 10 period affected)

**Documentation:**
- WIRED investigation (July 2025): "A technical error prevented roughly half of them from recording, including most inside the SHU."
- Source: 2023 government report (cited in WIRED)

### August 9, 2019: System Scheduled for Repairs

**Timeline:**
- **August 9, 2019 (night):** MCC system scheduled for repairs
- **Timing:** ONE NIGHT BEFORE Epstein death
- **Coincidence question:** Why scheduled for repair on this specific night?

**Issues:**
- Was repair notice sent in advance? (Would indicate system was known to be failing)
- Who authorized the repair schedule? (MCC administration? DOJ?)
- Was equipment already compromised (set up for failure)?

**Source:** WIRED investigation; CBS News reporting

### August 10, 2019: Final Failure & Death

**Timeline:**
- **~6:00 AM, August 10, 2019:** Final hard drive fails
- **~6:30 AM:** Alarm sounds; Epstein found dead in cell
- **Timing:** Hard drive failure coincides with death discovery (within ~30 minutes)

**The critical question:** 
- Was the final hard drive failure mechanical (normal degradation) or intentional (sabotage)?
- **No forensic analysis conducted** (as far as public record shows)

**Documentation:**
- Planet-Today report (Feb 2026): "July 29, 2019: The MCC's DVR motherboard fails... August 10, 2019 (~6:00 a.m.): The final hard drive fails right around the time the alarm sounds for Epstein's body."
- CBS News investigation (Jan 2026): Confirmed timeline

## SECTION 6: CRIMINAL LIABILITY ANALYSIS

### Possible Charges (If Negligence Proven)

**Obstruction of Justice (18 U.S.C. § 1503):**
- If evidence (camera video) intentionally destroyed
- If destruction prevented death investigation
- Penalty: Up to 20 years federal prison

**Deprivation of Rights Under Color of Law (18 U.S.C. § 242):**
- If MCC officials conspired to suppress evidence
- If constitutional rights (due process) violated
- Penalty: Up to 10 years federal prison

**Misprision of Felony (18 U.S.C. § 4) [Repealed 2016, but historical]:**
- If federal officials knew of evidence destruction, failed to report
- (Now prosecuted as conspiracy or obstruction instead)

### Possible Charges (If Intentional Sabotage Proven)

**Murder / Accomplice Liability:**
- If evidence destruction prevented investigation of homicide
- If officials conspired to enable/cover up death
- Penalty: Life imprisonment

**Conspiracy (18 U.S.C. § 371):**
- Multiple officials conspiring to obstruct death investigation
- Penalty: Up to 5 years federal prison per count
